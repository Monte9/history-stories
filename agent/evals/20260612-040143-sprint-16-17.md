**B3. 4 bodies: random assignment, B cycles, persists across reload — FAIL.**
What passes: fresh profiles get `museum.avatar.v1` randomly in 0-3 (got "2", then "1"; `s1617-b3-avatars.js` FRESH/FRESH2); B cycles 2→3→0→1→2 and writes localStorage; the localStorage value survives reload; the four looks are genuinely distinct — navy shirt/black hair (0), green shirt/dark skin/flat-top (1), maroon shirt/brown hair/blue jeans (2), mustard shirt/bald/darker skin/broader (3) (`crop-s17-b3-variant-*.png`); zero errors.
What fails: **the stored variant is never applied to the local body on load.** With `museum.avatar.v1=3` preset, the rendered shirt stays variant-0 navy [24,30,41] at +1 s, +3 s and +6 s after `data-loaded` (`s1617-b3b-reload.js`); after a full B cycle back to the stored value, reloading reverts the self-view to navy again while localStorage still holds the choice (`s17-b3-after-reload.png` shows navy with ls="3"). Worse, it splits self from remote: the wire `v` IS read from storage (B4 evidence), so after a reload your friends see your mustard body while you see yourself navy.
Root cause (for the fix, verified in artifact behavior): `src/components/museum/MuseumRoom.tsx` — `initVariant()` (line 537) fires its store notification inside an effect that runs BEFORE the `subscribeCamMode` listener effect (line 549) is registered, and `useState(playerStore.variant)` (line 548) captured the pre-init default 0 at first render. The update is missed; only a later `cycleVariant()` (B press) reaches the subscribed listener.
