# Curator taste audit 2 — the body and the company (sprints 12-15)

Date: 2026-06-12 02:19 UTC. Mode: curator taste audit per RUBRIC, sprint 16 opening. Not a verdict; a ranked shippability judgment of the third-person body and live company against Monte's goals: "I want to see my human body, especially as I walk" and "we should be able to walk around together." Driven live on localhost:3000 via Playwright/SwiftShader at 1280x900 and 390x844 (iPhone 12). Scripts: `agent/evals/tmp/ta2-*.js`. Screenshots: `agent/evals/shots/ta2-*.png` (+ `crop-ta2-*`). Peers simulated over `?net=local` with protocol-conformant BroadcastChannel senders, including moving peers with velocity-matched `s`; every pose claim is anchored to `#museum-hud` / `.museum-peer` reads logged at capture.

## Overall impression

The mechanics deserve their PASSes: the gait is genuinely alive at full speed, slow speed, backward, and in remote interpolation; materialize and teleport stage as fades (teleport fade-in caught on film, `ta2-tp-0.png`); the four-peer scene with realistic ids reads as four distinct, colorful people in a bright room (`ta2-group2-1280.png`); mobile holds with company and the sprint-14/15 layout fixes verifiably landed. The room itself still looks like the Broad from every wall with the body in frame. But this is not yet a clip Monte would proudly tweet of walking with a friend, for four reasons that all live in the exact filming path: spin around near a wall and the chase camera passes through wall/painting geometry, blacking out most of the frame while the HUD claims boom 3.50; stand at a wall facing the room (the natural "turn around and look at your friend" move) and the camera parks at 1.17 forever, your own half-faded back filling the frame and fully hiding a peer 20 u away; walk side by side and the buddy's "Visitor 2" label balloons to a third of the screen, burying the paintings; and every story round trip rebrands your friend as a new stranger (Visitor 5 becomes 6 becomes 7 even with an identical id). All four are bounded, verifiable fixes. Ship after those; the rest is polish.

## Ranked findings

**1. Chase camera passes through wall/painting geometry during sustained turns near walls; frame blacks out — blocks shippable.**
Evidence: `ta2-rp-turn-2.png` — mean luminance 47/255, ~60% of the frame solid black (backface/inside of east-wall painting geometry), HUD at capture: player (10.50, -2.77) pinned at the east wall, heading 357.4, `data-cam-dist` "3.50"; `ta2-rp-turn-7.png` (luma 125, black wedges, heading 339.2, dist 3.43); first seen un-staged in `ta2-turninplace-1.png`. Deterministic repro (`ta2-a4-replay.js`): pin the player on the east wall, hold ArrowLeft; the blackout occurs as the boom direction sweeps past wall-adjacent headings. The HUD reporting 3.50 while the rendered camera is inside geometry shows the smoothed camera lags the per-frame clamp; a slow static back-in (`ta2-pin-*.png`, sprint 12 AC5) never triggers it.
Suggested AC: with the player pinned against any wall, a held 360 degree turn (both key and drag) at 1280x900 yields no captured frame with mean luminance below 60% of the open-floor baseline and no frame showing through-wall or backface geometry; `data-cam-dist` matches the rendered camera's actual distance within 0.3 u at every sampled frame.

**2. Standing at a wall facing the room parks the boom at ~1.17: a giant half-faded body owns the frame and hides what you look at, including peers — blocks shippable.**
Evidence: `ta2-rp-idle0.png`/`ta2-rp-idle1.png` — heading 259.5, `data-cam-dist` "1.17" stable across 3.7 s at rest; `ta2-pin-2-d1_4.png` (dist 1.15, same composition backed into the south wall); worst case `ta2-label-far-1280.png` — observer at (0.00, 10.50) dist 1.21 looking at Visitor 2 at (0.00, -9.50): the peer is completely occluded by the player's own translucent back. This is a resting state reached by the most natural move in the room (walk to a wall, turn around), not a transient.
Suggested AC: with the player within 1.5 u of any wall facing the room interior, the local body occludes no more than ~15% of the 1280x900 frame (full fade-out below ~1.5 u boom, or camera raise/shoulder offset), and a peer standing 8+ u away on the sight line is visible and identifiable in the screenshot.

**3. Peer labels balloon at conversation distance, burying the scene — blocks shippable.**
Evidence: `ta2-together-1.png` + `crop-ta2-together-1.png` — buddy 1.3 u to the side mid-walk (peer (1.20, -5.14), player (0.00, -4.99), both speed ~3): the "Visitor 2" pill spans ~35% of the 1280 frame and covers an entire painting; `ta2-label-pointblank-1280.png` (~38% at 0.5 u); `ta2-firstperson-peer-1280.png` (first person at 1.5 u, label dominates the upper frame); `ta2-m-label-near-390.png` (38% of a 390 viewport). Far legibility is already right: at 17.75 u the label is small but readable (`ta2-label-far2-1280.png`, `crop-ta2-far2-center.png`) — only the near end is broken.
Suggested AC: with a peer 1-2.5 u away, the rendered label pill is at most ~15% of frame width at 1280x900 (~25% at 390) or fades out inside ~1.5 u, while remaining readable at 15+ u as today; verified by screenshot at peer distances 0.8, 2, 8, and 17 u.

**4. Visitor identity churns on every rejoin: a friend returns from a story as a different stranger — blocks shippable.**
Evidence: `ta2-b-company.js` numbering sequence — peer left and rejoined with a NEW id: relabeled Visitor 5 -> Visitor 6 (AFTER_CHURN_NEWID); the SAME id leaving (bye) and rejoining became Visitor 7 (AFTER_CHURN_SAMEID) — even byte-identical ids are renumbered. With real sessions the id changes across a story round trip (sprint 15 AC1), so the color churns too. Confirms sprint-13 note 3 / sprint-15 note 4, upgraded: story trips are the product's core loop, and "walking together" breaks when your companion's name and shirt change every time they read something.
Suggested AC: A and B see each other; B does Enter -> story -> Escape; on A, B re-materializes with the same label and color it had before the trip (and at most one node ever exists for B). Same for a reload within the session. Implies a stable per-browser identity (or label/color reuse keyed on it) carried in the protocol.

**5. Label pile-up in groups: overlapping pills merge into an unreadable smear — nice to have.**
Evidence: `ta2-group-4-1280.png` — three center-cluster labels overlap into one white strip; `ta2-group2-1280.png` — at wall distance the four labels visually fuse with painting placards and lower frame edges. Bodies stay individually readable (distinct tints, depths); only the text layer degrades. Confirms sprint-15 note 2.
Suggested AC: in a 4-peer cluster within a 4 u circle, every rendered label is either fully readable (no overlap with another label) or suppressed (e.g. nearest N labels only, or depth-staggered heights); no screenshot shows two labels intersecting.

**6. Peer color hash collapses on similar ids — nice to have.**
Evidence: `ta2-b-company.js` GROUP log — five sequential ids (`peer-aaa1`...`peer-eee5`) ALL hashed to `#5b7793`; five identical slate shirts in `ta2-group-4-1280.png`. Realistic random-hex ids spread correctly (`ta2-group2-1280.png`: #6b7f3a, #4f5d9e, #b85c38, #b08b2e). Real trystero ids are random, so impact is low, but the hash has near-zero avalanche; arrival-order-aware assignment (pick the least-used palette slot) would also cut real 1-in-8 twin-shirt collisions between two friends.
Suggested AC: any 4 simultaneously rendered peers receive 4 distinct palette colors regardless of id shape (collision only permitted at 9+ peers).

**7. V toggle is a hard cut between first and third — nice to have.**
Evidence: rAF sampling of `data-cam-dist` across the toggle (`ta2-a2-boom.js`): third -> first renders 0.00 on the first post-keypress sample with no intermediates; first -> third jumps 0.00 -> 3.50 between adjacent samples. The view teleports rather than pulling back over the shoulder.
Suggested AC: pressing V at rest produces at least 3 strictly intermediate `data-cam-dist` samples over a ~250-400 ms eased transition (disabled under prefers-reduced-motion), with no input dropped during it.

**8. Mannequin tells up close: sphere hands as big as fists, ball elbows, no neck — nice to have.**
Evidence: `ta2-crop-avatar-default.png` (2x of the default frame) — hands are bare skin spheres nearly forearm-width, elbow balls bulge, head sits directly on the shoulders; same reads in `crop-ta2-together-1.png` at buddy distance. The color blocking (skin/navy/black) carries the human read; the joints are what still says "wooden figure". Confirms sprint-12 note 2; the bare forearms reading as rolled-up sleeves is fine and can stay.
Suggested AC: at default boom in a 1280x900 screenshot, the hand reads narrower than the forearm (tapered or scaled hand geometry) and a neck segment separates head from torso, with gait and per-peer tinting unchanged.

**9. Narrow-desktop hint copy contradicts the visible controls — nice to have.**
Evidence: 390x844 desktop (fine pointer) context: hint reads "Arrow keys or scroll to walk · drag to look around · Enter to view a painting · Esc to step back" while the touch cluster is also on screen (`ta2-narrow-desktop-390.png`, NARROW_DESKTOP log: cluster:true). Confirms sprint-15 note 5.
Suggested AC: whenever `#museum-touch-controls` is mounted, the hint mentions the on-screen controls (the coarse-pointer copy), regardless of pointer type.

## Prior soft notes re-judged

- **Boom 3.50 vs SPEC ~3.1 — dismissed.** Composition at 3.50 is right: full figure, lower ~40% of frame, focused wall clear above (`ta2-solo-default-1280.png`, all four `ta2-face-*-1280.png`). Leave it.
- **Body fade curve near walls — escalated** into finding 2 (it is not just "could fade more"; the resting state occludes peers entirely).

- **No prompt on the spawn-focused painting while/after the hint — fixed.** The prompt appears the moment the hint dismisses with no focus change (PROMPT_AFTER_HINT_NO_REFOCUS: display flex, same slug). The MuseumRoom prompt now explicitly yields to the hint and recovers.
- **Near-camera label ballooning — confirmed, escalated** to finding 3 with cross-viewport evidence.
- **Label occlusion in groups — confirmed** as finding 5.
- **Visitor-number churn — confirmed and escalated** to finding 4 (same id renumbers too).
- **Ball-joint forearms — confirmed** as finding 8 (hands worse than forearms).
- **Chip/wordmark overlap at 390 — stays fixed** (`ta2-m-company-390.png`: chip "3 in the room" clean below the wordmark, no overlap, no overflow 390/390).
- **Entering fade ~750 ms vs spec 400 — dismissed.** Joins read as a materialize, never a pop (sprint-14 film + this audit's bursts); timing deviation has no felt cost.
- **Label slightly left of head at oblique angles — dismissed as standalone**; folded into the label work in findings 3/5.
