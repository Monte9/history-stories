## Sprint goal judgment

Monte asked to see his human body, especially while walking; named failure modes: sliding mannequin, floating capsule, turntable statue, mid-stride freeze. None present: the figure is grounded (feet on the floor in every shot), the gait alternates with knee flex and arm counter-swing synced to displacement, idle settles to a natural stance within a second, and turning in place shuffles the feet. The clothed color-blocking (skin head/hands, navy shirt, black trousers, shoes) makes it read as a person, not a wood mannequin, and it stays legible against the bright Broad-style room. Goal achieved, not just letter-passed.

## Soft notes (for the sprint 16 taste pass)

- Default boom is 3.50 vs SPEC 11.3's "~3.1"; composition is good (body ~32% of frame height) so this is tuning headroom, not a defect.
- The forearm/hand capsules in bare skin read as rolled-up sleeves; ball-joint elbows/hands are visible at point-blank boom. A wrist taper or sleeve tone on the forearms would raise fidelity cheaply.
- At pinned boom (1.15) the faded body is still fairly prominent (torso at 58,63,71 over the wall). A stronger fade curve below ~1.0 would clear the frame more decisively.
- First-visit hint only dismisses on keypress after it has mounted; a key pressed in the first ~2s leaves it up (seen once in an early-run screenshot). Harmless, worth a debounce.
- Focus outline tint follows tradition color correctly (amber on Roman, emerald on the curator wall's Ramayana piece) and is legible against white.
