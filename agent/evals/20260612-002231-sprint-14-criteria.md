# Sprint 14 — Agreed acceptance criteria (aligned 2026-06-12, pre-build)

Sprint goal: company becomes visible — remote visitors render as tinted `AvatarBody` figures with `Visitor N` billboard labels, staged entering/live/leaving fades, interpolation-driven gait, the >3 u teleport rule, and the `#museum-presence-count` chip (SPEC 12.4-12.5, gait per 11.2, DOM contract per 12.6). Monte's bar: "see them materialize in the same room and walk around together" — the body must feel alive, not a sliding statue or a popping ghost.

Verification context: all checks over `?net=local` with 2-3 Playwright pages in ONE browser context. Sprint 13 already proved transport correctness (discovery, streaming, despawn, ghost-proofing); these criteria do not re-litigate it. SwiftShader runs 2-10 fps with two WebGL rooms: effective DOM-sample cadence is ~300 ms (sprint 13 measured maxStep 0.66-1.23 u at ~300-317 ms), so all motion checks are displacement-anchored with generous windows, and screenshot evidence is paired with the `data-peer-*` values recorded at capture time. Both pages spawn at the same default point; separate them (walk or `?face=`) before any composition screenshot.

## Agreed acceptance criteria

1. **Company is visible and identifiable from across the room.** Pages A and B at `/?net=local`, separated by >= 8 u: B's 1280x900 screenshot shows a full articulated human figure (head, torso, arms, legs — not a capsule or partial body) standing where A actually is — the figure's rendered spot matches B's `data-peer-x`/`-z`, which match A's own `data-x`/`-z` within 0.5 u. Its shirt/accent tint is clearly distinct from the local charcoal body, and a `Visitor 2` billboard pill floats above its head, matching `data-peer-label` and readable in the screenshot against the white wall at that distance. With a third page C joined: the primary page shows `data-peers="2"` and two figures with distinct labels (`Visitor 2` / `Visitor 3`) and visibly different tints from each other AND from charcoal (on the spec-accepted 1-in-8 hash collision, reload one peer to re-roll and re-shoot).

2. **Materialize and vanish are staged — never pops.** On join, the peer's `data-peer-state` passes `entering` then `live`, with `entering` persisting across more than one sample (an instant attribute flip fails). On page close, `leaving` is observed before the node is removed, and the figure, its `.museum-peer` node, and the count chip are all gone within 5 s, `data-peers` back to `"0"`. At least one of the two transitions is also caught visually: a screenshot taken during an `entering` or `leaving` window shows the figure partially faded/scaled (not at full presence) — the mesh must fade, not just the attribute.

3. **The walk reads alive: interpolated motion drives the gait.** While A walks a straight path displacing >= 4 u, B's rendered pose (`data-peer-x`/`-z`, MutationObserver or ~100-200 ms poll; cadence may degrade to ~300 ms): at least 6 distinct intermediates strictly between endpoints, no sampled step > 1.5 u, progress along the walk direction never regresses by more than 0.15 u, settling within 0.3 u of A's final pose within ~2 s of A stopping. Gait, displacement-anchored: two screenshots whose recorded `data-peer-x`/`-z` differ by 0.3-0.6 u show the figure in visibly different limb configurations with at least one clearly mid-stride (feet separated or foot lifted) — identical poses across that displacement is the gliding-statue FAIL. After settle, a screenshot shows a standing idle (both feet grounded, legs together, arms hanging — not frozen mid-stride) and the figure stops translating (< 0.1 u drift over 2 s). Turning: A turning ~180° in place is reflected in B's `data-peer-heading` within ~15° (mod 360) and a screenshot shows the body facing the new way.

4. **The teleport rule: discontinuities relocate, never glide.** Produce a same-session pose jump > 3 u (e.g. suppress A's outgoing updates for ~3-4 s — comfortably below the despawn timeout — while A relocates >= 6 u, then restore; any equivalent in-band method is fine). During the gap, B's figure freezes in place (no extrapolated drift > 0.3 u) and `data-peers` holds `"1"`. When the far state arrives, the figure relocates without a cross-room glide: at most one sampled `data-peer-x`/`-z` lies in the interior of the jump (> 1 u from both endpoints), the relocation completes within ~1.5 s of the first post-gap update, and it is staged — a screenshot or a transitional `data-peer-state` shows the figure faded/absent mid-relocation rather than sliding. Peer id/label/color are unchanged across the whole maneuver and no duplicate node ever appears.

5. **The chip counts honestly; presence stays scenery.** `#museum-presence-count` is visible on a page only while rendered peers >= 1: absent on a solo `/?net=local` load, appears when a peer joins, its displayed number increases when a second peer joins, and it disappears within ~5 s of the last peer leaving. At `/?net=off` no chip, no `.museum-peer`, and no remote mesh ever appears. Non-interference: with the remote figure standing directly in the local player's line of sight to a painting, gaze focus still acquires that painting (`data-focused` set, `#museum-prompt` visible) and Enter opens it; walking straight through the remote body is unobstructed (continuous `data-x`/`-z` progress, no stall); incoming peer traffic never alters local `data-x`/`-z`/`-heading`/`data-focused`.

6. **Both camera modes, both viewports, nothing regresses.** `pnpm build` exits 0. In first person (`?cam=first` or `V`), the remote figure is visible in a screenshot while no part of YOUR body shows; toggling back to third shows both bodies. At 390x844 with a peer present: no horizontal overflow, the touch cluster still moves the player, and the remote figure plus chip render. Zero console errors across every local-mode page in these sessions at both viewports; homepage, one story page, and `/gallery` still render correctly per the standing gates.

## Changes vs the draft (rationale, one line each)

- **Draft 1 → AC 1 (strengthened):** pinned the >= 8 u separation and across-the-room label legibility (SPEC 12.4 says legible from across the room, and both pages spawn on the same point), added figure-position truthfulness against A's real HUD, and added a third page so tints are proven distinguishable from EACH OTHER, not only from charcoal.
- **Draft 2 → AC 2 (strengthened):** the draft's "screenshots OR state attributes" let an implementation set `entering` for 400 ms while visually popping the mesh; now states must persist across samples AND at least one transition must be caught mid-fade in a screenshot.
- **Draft 3 → AC 3 (recalibrated + sharpened):** "no step > 0.5 u at 100 ms" is unmeasurable at the sandbox's ~300 ms effective cadence (sprint 13: maxStep 0.66-1.23 u), so the bound is re-anchored on displacement (>= 6 intermediates, <= 1.5 u steps, monotonic progress, bounded settle); "legs visibly animating" became displacement-anchored screenshot pairs (0.3-0.6 u apart, ~quarter gait cycle) that catch a gliding statue; "limb poses stop changing" replaced with a grounded-idle-stance check, since SPEC 11.2's idle breathes (the draft would fail a correct idle and pass a frozen mid-stride).
- **Draft 4 → folded into AC 3:** turning is the same aliveness property as walking; merging freed the slot the teleport rule needs.
- **NEW AC 4 (added):** the teleport rule is in this sprint's scope line and SPEC 12.3 but no draft tested it; a cross-room glide on a >3 u jump is exactly the dead-puppet artifact Monte would notice, and the freeze-during-gap rule (extrapolate <= 250 ms then idle) gets pinned here too.
- **Draft 5 → AC 5 (sharpened):** chip now must count correctly as peers change (increase on a second join, vanish after the last leave), and "never block focus" became an explicit through-the-body focus-and-Enter test instead of an assertion.
- **Draft 6 → AC 6 (kept, pinned):** first person specified as "remote visible, own limbs absent, toggle back shows both" with screenshot evidence, plus the standing build/overflow/console/regression gates.
