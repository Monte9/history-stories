# Sprint 13 — Agreed acceptance criteria (aligned 2026-06-11, pre-build)

Sprint goal: presence plumbing — `PresenceTransport`, the `?net=local` BroadcastChannel reference transport, the trystero/Nostr `webrtc` default (lazy, silent degrade to `off`), `?net=off`, the state/bye protocol with tick rate and heartbeat timeout, and the HUD presence contract (SPEC 12.2-12.3, 12.6). No remote bodies render; the deliverable is provably flowing, ghost-free peer state that sprint 14 can build bodies on without protocol rework.

Verification context: all `local`-mode checks use two (or three) Playwright pages in ONE browser context (BroadcastChannel requires same origin + same context). The sandbox may block all egress, so the production-path criterion must settle to a definite observable state either way. SwiftShader runs 2-10 fps under load: sampling checks use displacement anchors and generous windows, not tight wall-clock guarantees.

## Agreed acceptance criteria

1. **Local discovery, honest contract, no self-echo.** A single page at `/?net=local` settles to `data-net="local"` with `data-peers="0"` and no `.museum-peer` nodes (a tab never counts itself). Opening a second page in the same browser context at `/?net=local`: within ~5 s both pages show `data-peers="1"` with exactly one `.museum-peer` node carrying all seven contract attributes — `data-peer-id` non-empty, `data-peer-label` reading `Visitor 2` on each side, `data-peer-color` a CSS hex color, `data-peer-x`/`-z`/`-heading` numeric to 2 decimals, `data-peer-state` present. At rest, each page's `data-peer-x`/`-z`/`-heading` match the other page's own `data-x`/`-z`/`-heading` within 0.5 u / 10°. `data-peer-id`, `-label`, and `-color` for a given peer are unchanged when re-sampled after criterion 2's walk (identity is stable for the session).

2. **Live streaming both ways, smooth enough for bodies.** While page A walks a path displacing >= 3 u, page B's `data-peer-x`/`-z` (sampled every ~100-200 ms) pass through at least 5 distinct intermediate positions strictly between A's start and end, with no sampled step > 1.5 u; within ~2 s of A stopping they settle within 0.3 u of A's final `data-x`/`data-z`. A turning in place is reflected in B's `data-peer-heading` to within ~10° (compared modulo 360). The reverse direction (B walks, A observes) shows the same streaming behavior in at least one spot check.

3. **Leaves, timeouts, and ghost-proofing.** (a) Closing page B removes its `.museum-peer` node from page A within 5 s and `data-peers` returns to `"0"`. (b) A peer whose tab dies WITHOUT a clean unload (e.g. a crashed renderer — no `bye` sent) is removed by heartbeat timeout within ~10 s of its last update. (c) Reloading page A three times in quick succession leaves B settled at exactly `data-peers="1"` with one node whose `data-peer-id` is A's current session id, and at no sampled moment does `data-peers` exceed the number of live tabs. (d) Two pages left idle (no input) for 12+ s both keep `data-peers="1"` — idle heartbeats prevent false despawn.

4. **The production default settles honestly and silently.** Loading the room with no `?net` param: within ~15 s of `data-loaded="true"`, `data-net` reads exactly `"webrtc"` or `"off"` (never absent, blank, or an intermediate value), and over a further ~30 s in the room there are zero console errors and zero unhandled promise rejections — including when relays/WebRTC are unreachable, as in this sandbox (at most one info-level console message about presence). In either outcome the solo room behaves identically to sprint 12: `data-peers="0"`, walking, focus, Enter to open a story, and Escape back all work.

5. **Presence costs nothing where it isn't wanted.** At `/?net=off`: `data-net="off"`, `data-peers` stays `"0"`, no `.museum-peer` node ever appears, and the page makes zero presence-related network requests (no relay websockets, no STUN/TURN). On the default (no-param) load, any JS not fetched under `?net=off` is fetched only after the room reports `data-loaded="true"` (the presence chunk is lazy). `/gallery`, one story page, and the no-WebGL fallback open zero presence network connections in any mode and log zero console errors.

6. **Nothing shipped regresses.** `pnpm build` exits 0. With a peer connected at `/?net=local`, the sprint-12 solo journey still passes on page A: third-person default, keyboard movement drives `data-x`/`-z`/`-heading`, wall-painting focus shows `#museum-prompt`, Enter opens the story, Escape returns to the same pose; the HUD's player attributes are never altered by incoming peer traffic. Zero console errors in the room at 1280x900 and 390x844, and no horizontal overflow at 390px.

## Changes vs the draft (rationale, one line each)

- **Draft 1 → AC 1 (strengthened):** added the solo `data-peers="0"` precondition (self-echo would otherwise pass), pose truthfulness against the other page's real HUD values, attribute well-formedness, and session-stable id/label/color — sprint 14 needs identity that doesn't flap.
- **Draft 2 → AC 2 (kept, recalibrated):** displacement anchor (>= 3 u walk, >= 5 intermediates) and a 1.5 u step ceiling replace the tight "no gap > 1 u at 100 ms" that SwiftShader stalls could flake; added the B→A reverse spot check so symmetry is proven, and bounded the settle window.
- **Draft 3 → AC 3 (strengthened):** closing a tab fires `bye`, so the draft never exercised the heartbeat timeout at all; added the no-bye death (crash) removed by timeout, the rapid-reload ghost loop with a never-overcounts invariant, and idle-heartbeat persistence so quiet peers don't false-despawn.
- **Draft 4 → AC 4 (strengthened):** bounded the settle time (~15 s after load), forbade absent/intermediate `data-net` values (the attribute must not lie), counted unhandled promise rejections alongside console errors over a 30 s watch, and pinned "at most one info-level message" per SPEC 12.2.
- **Draft 5 → split into AC 5 and AC 6:** the draft folded opt-out and regression together and skipped SPEC 12.2/12.7 scope rules entirely; AC 5 adds the observable lazy-load check (no extra JS before `data-loaded`) and the never-loads-presence guarantee for `/gallery`, story pages, and the no-WebGL fallback.
- **AC 6 (kept from draft 5's regression half, sharpened):** regression is now exercised WITH a live peer attached (peer traffic must not perturb the local player's HUD attributes), not just solo, plus the standard build/overflow/console gates.
- **Cut:** nothing wholesale; draft content was redistributed, and remote-body or rejoin-after-story checks were deliberately left to sprints 14/15 where they are in scope.
