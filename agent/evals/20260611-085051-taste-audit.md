# Curator Taste Audit — Sprint 10 input

Audited 2026-06-11 (UTC) per RUBRIC "Curator taste audit". Dev server localhost:3000, `pnpm build` green (exit 0, 15/15 pages). Playwright chromium (swiftshader), 1280x900 desktop and iPhone 12 390x844. Walked the room from spawn, visited all four `?face=` walls plus close approaches, did the full story round trip (Enter, carousel, Escape; position restored dx=0.000 dz=0.000), and repeated essentials on touch (hold-forward walk 3.6 units, tap-to-focus, prompt tap opened story, visible back button restored position). Zero console errors in every run (desktop walk, story trip, ceiling probe, mobile walk). Scratch scripts: `agent/evals/tmp/audit10-*.js`. Screenshots: `agent/evals/shots/audit-*.png`.

## Overall impression

Not shippable today, but close, and the gap is finish, not structure. The bones are genuinely good: the honeycomb veil reads beautifully in perspective (audit-ceiling-pitchup-1280.png), motion feel is the strongest part of the build (walk a steady 3 u/s with no frame jumps, turn 100°/s, wheel impulse 0.88 units with a clean ~700 ms decay, drag look responsive with pitch clamped exactly at ±25), gaze focus is reliable near and far, and the story-page triptych is tasteful. But a visitor holding The Broad photo next to this room would not nod yet, for one big reason and several small ones. The big one: the value relationships are inverted. In the reference, the walls are the brightest, whitest surfaces and the veil glows above them; here the walls render mid-gray RGB(192,191,187) while the ceiling cells hit 226, so the room reads as a gray utility corridor under a nice ceiling rather than a luminous white gallery. Add a dead-flat floor with zero sheen, and the bottom two-thirds of every wide shot is gray-on-gray. The smaller ones are sloppiness a demanding visitor hits in the first 10 seconds: the first-visit hint and the focus prompt render on top of each other at spawn, the "Gallery view" pill is unreadable over the bright room, and raw `*asterisks*` sit in four story bodies. Fix findings 1-5 and this crosses the bar.

## Ranked findings

### 1. Walls render mid-gray, not gallery white — the core reference miss
- Area: lighting. Severity: **blocks shippable**.
- Evidence: 5x5 pixel samples of bare wall in audit-close-roman-1280.png, audit-face-curator/ramayana/mahabharata-1280.png all read RGB(192,191,187) (#c0bfbb, 75% value) while ceiling cells read 226 (audit10-crops.js output). Mean shot luminance is fine (176-184) but the wall surface itself is dingier than the ceiling, the opposite of The Broad, where walls are near-white and the veil glows above them.
- Fix: in `src/components/museum/MuseumRoom.tsx`, raise the light rig and/or lift the wall material color so a bare wall area renders near-white without blowing out paintings. AC: in fresh 1280x900 screenshots at all four `?face=` walls, a 5x5 mean sample of bare wall (e.g. 200,200 in a close approach) is RGB ≥ 225 and ≤ 248 per channel, and the painting canvases remain unclipped (placard cards sampled ≤ 250).

### 2. First-visit hint and focus prompt render on top of each other at spawn
- Area: spacing/composition. Severity: **blocks shippable**.
- Evidence: at desktop first load, `#museum-hint` box (320,806 640x62) and `#museum-prompt` box (402,826 475x42) overlap (audit10-overlap.js, overlap:true, both visible); the prompt sits entirely inside the hint, garbling the instructions to "Arrow keys... Esc to st—" behind the prompt pill (audit-spawn-hint-1280.png, audit-crop-hint-bar.png). Gaze focus sets `data-focused` immediately at spawn, so this is every new visitor's first frame.
- Fix: in the museum overlay layout (`src/components/museum/MuseumClient.tsx`), stack them (prompt above hint with ≥ 12px gap) or suppress the prompt while the hint is showing. AC: on a fresh profile at 1280x900 with a painting focused at spawn, both elements' bounding rects are disjoint (or the prompt is hidden until the hint dismisses), verified by getBoundingClientRect and screenshot.

### 3. "Gallery view" pill is illegible over the bright room at both viewports
- Area: typography/cohesion. Severity: **blocks shippable**.
- Evidence: computed style is color rgb(136,136,160) on bg oklab(0 0 0 / 0.4) (audit10-overlap.js); composited over the bright wall/ceiling the pill becomes mid-gray on mid-gray, roughly 1.1:1 text contrast. Crops audit-crop-pill-on-wall.png and audit-crop-pill-on-ceiling.png show the text nearly vanishing; same on mobile (audit-m-face-roman-390.png top right). This is the primary fallback/nav link on `/`. The adjacent "HISTORY STORIES" pill (rgb(240,240,245) text) is fine, proving the fix.
- Fix: in `src/app/page.tsx`, give the Gallery view link the same light text as the h1 pill and a solid dark pill (e.g. rgba(10,10,15,0.7)). AC: computed text color vs pill background contrast ≥ 4.5:1, and 1280 + 390 screenshots show the label legible over both ceiling and wall.

### 4. Literal markdown asterisks render in story body text
- Area: typography/copy. Severity: **blocks shippable**.
- Evidence: DOM text scan of all 11 story pages (audit10-story.js BODY_ISSUES): `*kavacha*`, `*kundala*`, `*Daanveer Karna*`, `*Vasava Shakti*` (the-armor-karna-gave-away), `*Meditations*` (marcus-aurelius), `*Phaedo*` (man-who-read-plato), `*that's still you*` (god-who-forgot). The markdown renderer is not parsing emphasis; raw markup in body copy on a content site reads as broken.
- Fix: in the story body renderer (`src/app/[slug]/page.tsx` or its markdown lib), parse `*text*` to `<em>text</em>`. AC: a DOM scan of every story page's article text finds zero `*...*` pairs, and `/20260328-014232-the-armor-karna-gave-away` renders kavacha/kundala inside `<em>` elements (screenshot of the paragraph).

### 5. Default spawn composition is sparse: tiny far paintings over a half-frame of empty floor
- Area: composition. Severity: **blocks shippable**.
- Evidence: spawn is (x -9.5, z 0) facing the Ramayana wall 21.5 units away; in audit-spawn-1280.png the five paintings subtend roughly 60px of a 900px frame (~7%) while empty floor and blank wall fill the middle two-thirds. Worse in portrait (audit-m-spawn-hint-390.png). The Broad's generosity comes from large canvases at moderate distance, not small ones far away; first impression here is "empty room", not "monumental gallery".
- Fix: move the spawn closer (x ≈ -4 to -6, same heading) and/or scale paintings up (~3.8w x 2.375h, y center ~2.1). AC: at default spawn 1280x900, the facing wall's center painting measures ≥ 100px tall in the screenshot, and the room still passes collision/gaze regressions.

### 6. Floor is dead flat: no polished-concrete sheen, no reflection of the veil
- Area: lighting. Severity: nice to have (but the single biggest remaining vibe gap after 1).
- Evidence: floor samples near vs far differ by 4 levels (156 vs 160, audit10-crops.js) — a featureless matte plane occupying the bottom ~45% of every wide shot (audit-face-curator-1280.png, audit-face-roman-1280.png). The reference floor has a subtle sheen with soft bright pooling under the skylight.
- Fix: in `MuseumRoom.tsx` floor material, drop roughness to ~0.2-0.3 with slight metalness/clearcoat, or composite a soft radial gloss gradient into the floor texture echoing the veil glow. AC: in a `?face=` screenshot, a vertical sample column down the floor shows a luminance range ≥ 20 levels (visible sheen gradient), with the seam hairlines retained.

### 7. 3D placard text renders washed mid-gray instead of crisp dark-on-white
- Area: typography. Severity: nice to have.
- Evidence: audit-crop-placard-roman.png and audit-crop-acq-placard.png show title text at roughly #7a7a7a on a gray-lit card; the spec calls for ~#2b2b2b on ~#fbfaf7. Lighting multiplies the texture, dimming both card and ink; at normal viewing distance (audit-close-roman-1280.png) titles are hard to read.
- Fix: render placards (and the curator placard) with an unlit material (`meshBasicMaterial`) in `Painting.tsx`/`textures.ts`, or darken the drawn ink to compensate. AC: close-up screenshot 5x5 samples: placard ink ≤ RGB 90, card ≥ 235.

### 8. Ceiling veil glow is muted; cells do not read as skylight
- Area: lighting. Severity: nice to have.
- Evidence: cell interiors and webbing both sample ~226 at the top band of audit-face-roman-1280.png; in audit-ceiling-pitchup-1280.png the structure reads nicely but as an off-white textured ceiling, not "soft skylight glowing through each cell". Contrast between glowing interior and cell wall is too low to sell the light source.
- Fix: in `textures.ts` honeycomb generator, push cell interiors to ≥ #f8f8f5 with a radial falloff and deepen webbing toward ~#cccbc6. AC: in a `?face=` screenshot top band, cell-interior sample ≥ 243 and webbing sample ≤ 215.

### 9. Three-painting walls scatter edge to edge with dead wall between
- Area: composition. Severity: nice to have.
- Evidence: audit-face-mahabharata-1280.png and audit-crop-focused-vs-neighbor.png: adjacent frames on 3-painting walls sit roughly two painting-widths apart, reading as gaps in the collection rather than generous hanging. The curator wall is also lopsided: placard far left, three acquisitions far right, blank center (audit-face-curator-1280.png).
- Fix: in `layout.ts`, cap inter-painting gap (e.g. group total width ≤ 60% of wall, centered) for walls with ≤ 4 paintings, and center the curator wall grouping. AC: in `?face=mahabharata` at 1280, the horizontal gap between adjacent frames measures ≤ 1.2 painting widths in the screenshot.

### 10. "Esc" keyboard chip on the story page on phones
- Area: copy. Severity: nice to have.
- Evidence: on the iPhone 12 descriptor, the back link renders "← Back to the museum Esc" (audit10-mobile.js M_STORY_META, coarse:true; audit-m-story-top-390.png). A keyboard chip on a touch device is incongruous; the museum overlays already switch copy on coarse pointers.
- Fix: hide the chip under the same coarse-pointer condition in the story page back link component. AC: on a coarse-pointer 390x844 story page, the back link text contains no "Esc"; at desktop 1280 the chip remains.

### 11. Loading is a hard cut from black to full daylight, not a reveal
- Area: motion. Severity: nice to have.
- Evidence: frames sampled every 220 ms on first load (audit-load-00..03.png): three frames of pure black "ENTERING THE MUSEUM…" then the fully bright room, with no intermediate frame ever captured. The intended dark-to-light reveal currently reads as a flash-bang.
- Fix: once `data-loaded="true"`, fade the loading plate's opacity over ~600 ms (CSS transition on the overlay), optionally brightening the plate first. AC: screenshots at 100 ms intervals across the transition capture at least 2 frames whose mean luminance lies strictly between the plate's (<40) and the room's (>150).

### 12. Curator placard frame is visibly heavier than the painting frames
- Area: composition. Severity: nice to have.
- Evidence: audit-crop-curator-placard.png shows a thick black band around the placard versus the thin profiles on paintings (audit-crop-frame-detail.png); it reads as a different furniture system. The violet italic "A Walking Gallery" subtitle is also the loudest type on the wall.
- Fix: match the placard frame to the painting frame profile (~0.07 border) in `Painting.tsx`/`textures.ts` and mute the subtitle to the placard ink color. AC: in a curator close-up screenshot, placard frame thickness measures ≤ 1.5x the painting frame thickness.
