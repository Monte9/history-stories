# Sprint 15 — Agreed acceptance criteria (aligned 2026-06-12, pre-build)

Sprint goal: presence hardening — the edge rules that make presence trustworthy in real use (SPEC 12.3). Story-round-trip rejoin, long-idle stability, teleport-on-reload, a handful of concurrent visitors with distinct identities, the 8-peer render cap, and mobile usability with company present. Monte's "walk around together" includes reading together: a museum where opening a story orphans you from your friends is broken.

Two defects already on record shape these criteria. (1) Escape-return from a story strips query params: a `?net=local` page silently reverts to the default transport and never rejoins its peer, and a `?net=off` opt-out or `?cam=` choice is forgotten (sprint 13 verdict, soft notes 1-2). The draft rejoin AC is impossible as written until this is fixed; AC1/AC2 pin the fix's observable contract without naming a mechanism. (2) `#museum-presence-count` renders behind the wordmark at 390px (sprint 14 verdict, soft note 1); AC6 promotes the fix to a criterion.

Verification context (sandbox-bounded, agreed up front):
- SwiftShader runs 2-10 fps per WebGL page and a prior 4-page session OOM-crashed. **At most 3 full WebGL museum pages are open simultaneously** in any check. Peer identities beyond that are supplied by protocol-conformant senders on the documented local channel (`BroadcastChannel("museum.presence.local")`, SPEC 12.2) from lightweight same-origin pages. Equivalence: SPEC 12.2 defines the local transport as the reference implementation of the 12.3 protocol, and the observer's receive pipeline cannot distinguish sender provenance; receiver-side behaviors (identity assignment, cap, timeouts) are exactly what is under test, and real end-to-end sender behavior is covered by the two real pages here plus sprints 13-14. The evaluator sniffs a real page's wire format on the channel before synthesizing, and one retry is allowed if a page crashes mid-run.
- Background-tab state is produced with `page.bringToFront()` on the observer; Chromium throttles background-tab timers (~1 Hz), which is precisely the real-use condition AC3 must survive (heartbeat 2 s vs receiver timeout 6 s).
- All motion/cadence bounds stay displacement-anchored with generous windows (sprint 13 note 4: effective DOM cadence ~300 ms under SwiftShader).

## Agreed acceptance criteria

1. **The story round trip rejoins the room — both directions, no ghosts.** Pages A and B at `/?net=local`, mutually visible (`data-peers="1"` each side). A focuses a painting, Enter to the story, Escape back. On B, sampled (~300 ms) across the whole journey: `data-peers` only ever reads `"0"` or `"1"` and at most one `.museum-peer` node exists at any moment — one human is never two; if A's presence despawns during the trip it does so cleanly (`leaving` observed or node gone within ~10 s of the story opening; an implementation that holds A's identity through the trip without despawning is equally acceptable); within ~10 s of A's room reaching `data-loaded="true"` again, B shows exactly one node for A, staged in per the lifecycle (`entering` then `live`, never a pop), at A's restored pose (matching A's HUD within 0.5 u), and that node tracks A's subsequent walk (fresh intermediates, settling within 0.5 u). On A after Escape: `data-net` reads `"local"`, B re-appears as exactly one node bearing B's current session id, and B's live movement streams to A. After the return, A opens zero relay/WebSocket connections and emits zero exempt-class console noise — the choice really persisted; A did not bounce through the production transport. A second consecutive round trip behaves identically with no count creep on either side.

2. **Transport memory has exactly one shape: explicit param wins, the round trip inherits, fresh visits get the default.** (a) Escape-return inherits the session's settled choice: a `/?net=off` session that opens a story and Escapes back reads `data-net="off"`, `data-peers="0"`, no chip, and zero presence network requests after return (the opt-out is not forgotten); the `local` case is proven by AC1; a `?cam=first` session is still first-person (`data-cam-mode="first"`) after its own round trip, with the sprint-12 pose restore intact. (b) An explicit `?net=` param always wins on any direct load: `/?net=local` settles `"local"` and `/?net=off` settles `"off"` regardless of any earlier session in the browser. (c) Absent a param, a fresh visit — a new tab/navigation to bare `/` in the same browser context that just ran a `local` session — settles per the sprint-13 production rule (`"webrtc"` or honest `"off"`), never a remembered `"local"`. The story round trip is the only inheritance path.

3. **Long idle in a background tab is rock-solid; closing it still cleans up.** A (foregrounded, observing) and B at `/?net=local`, mutually visible; B then receives no input and stays backgrounded for >= 60 s. On A, sampled ~1 s for the full window: `data-peers="1"` at every sample, B's node keeps the same `data-peer-id` with `data-peer-state="live"` throughout (never flickers through `leaving`/`entering`), total pose drift < 0.1 u, and a screenshot near the 60 s mark shows the figure in the grounded idle stance — an idle background tab must never false-despawn on the foreground page. At the end both pages still report each other (`data-peers="1"` both sides). Closing B while still backgrounded: A removes the node and chip within ~10 s (`data-peers="0"`), and a further 15 s watch shows nothing re-materializing and zero console errors.

4. **Reload-teleport: a far respawn relocates, never glides and never doubles.** With B watching A's figure on screen and A >= 5 u from the mahabharata spawn, A reloads at `/?face=mahabharata&net=local`. On B at ~100-300 ms sampling: `data-peers` never exceeds `"1"` and two nodes never coexist; the old node despawns cleanly (gone within ~10 s); the new node's first rendered pose is within 1 u of A's post-reload HUD spawn and arrives staged (`entering` then `live`, fade not pop); no sampled `data-peer-x`/`-z` from either node lies in the interior of the old-to-new segment (> 1.5 u from both endpoints) — no cross-room slide; a screenshot shows the figure at the new wall. A new session id/label/color after reload is acceptable (SPEC 12.4; numbering churn is a sprint-16 taste item, not a defect here).

5. **A handful at once, and the cap accounts honestly.** Observer O at `/?net=local` with four concurrent peer identities — at least two of them real `/?net=local` museum pages, the rest protocol-conformant senders per the verification context, and at least two identities joining within ~1 s of each other to exercise the arrival-order race. O shows `data-peers="4"`, four `.museum-peer` nodes with four DISTINCT `data-peer-label` values, each `data-peer-color` a palette hex (hash collisions acceptable per SPEC 12.4), the chip reading "5 in the room", and a 1280x900 screenshot with four labeled figures placed apart. While one peer streams movement, O holds forward for 3-4 s: >= 2 u displacement, no sampled `data-x` stall >= 1.5 s, and the moving peer keeps streaming (>= 3 fresh intermediates) — company never costs control. Then raise to nine concurrent identities: `data-peers` reads exactly `"8"` (SPEC 12.6 — it counts rendered avatars, so it caps at 8 and never exceeds it) with exactly 8 nodes and zero console errors; the chip must not silently understate the room — it either counts everyone present (e.g. "10 in the room") or visibly marks overflow (e.g. "8+ in the room"); a plain number below the true population fails. Stop one RENDERED peer's sender: within ~10 s the previously unrendered ninth identity's node appears (staged) and `data-peers` returns to `"8"` — surplus peers are tracked and promoted, not dropped.

6. **Mobile holds with company present; solo and the site regress nowhere.** At 390x844 on `/?net=local` with a peer rendered: no horizontal overflow; the touch cluster still walks the player (measurable displacement); and at every observed state the bounding boxes of `#museum-presence-count`, the wordmark/header pill, the nav pills, the hint overlay (while visible), `#museum-prompt` (while a painting is focused), and the touch cluster are pairwise non-intersecting, with the chip's text fully legible in the 390 screenshot — the sprint-14 chip-under-wordmark overlap is now a FAIL. Solo: `/?net=off` and the settled production default behave identically to sprint 12 (walk, focus + prompt, Enter, Escape with pose restore), with zero `.museum-peer` ever appearing. `pnpm build` exits 0; zero console errors on every page in these sessions at both viewports (the sprint-13 exempt class applies only on the production-default settling path); homepage, one story page, and `/gallery` render correctly per the standing gates.

## Changes vs the draft (rationale, one line each)

- **Draft 1 → AC 1 + AC 2 (split and made possible):** the draft is unrunnable against the known param-stripping defect, so AC1 pins the rejoin journey's full observable shape (never-two-nodes, staged re-entry at the restored pose, re-acquisition both ways, no production-transport noise after return, a second trip with no creep) while AC2 pins the persistence contract the fix must satisfy — Escape-return inherits the settled choice (including the `?net=off` opt-out and `?cam=first`), an explicit param always wins, and a fresh paramless visit gets the production default so the fix cannot over-persist — all without naming a storage mechanism.
- **Draft 2 → AC 3 (strengthened to the real threat):** a foreground-idle test would pass trivially; browsers throttle background-tab timers, so the 60 s idle peer must be BACKGROUNDED and the foreground observer must hold `live` at every 1 s sample with a stable id and < 0.1 u drift (no flicker cycles), and the close-despawn bound widens 5 s → ~10 s to admit the heartbeat-timeout path if throttling drops the bye.
- **Draft 3 → AC 4 (reframed honestly):** a reload is a new session, not a same-id state jump (sprint 14 AC4 already proved that mechanism), so "repositions without gliding" becomes the observable that matters — clean old-node despawn, staged materialize within 1 u of the new spawn, a ban on any sampled interior position, and `data-peers` never exceeding 1 during the swap.
- **Draft 4 → AC 5 first half + AC 6 mobile half (bounded to what the sandbox can run):** 5 simultaneous WebGL pages risks the documented OOM crash, so the criterion fixes >= 2 real pages (<= 3 WebGL pages open, sprint-14's proven envelope) plus reference-protocol senders for the rest with equivalence justified and one crash retry; adds the 4-DISTINCT-labels requirement under a near-simultaneous join (the arrival-order race), pins the chip at "5 in the room", and makes responsiveness displacement-anchored (>= 2 u, no >= 1.5 s stall) per the SwiftShader calibration.
- **Draft 5 → AC 5 second half + AC 6 (cap made an honest-accounting test):** "peers beyond the cap are ignored" is too weak — SPEC 12.3 says tracked, so the agreed rule is `data-peers` reads exactly `"8"` with > 8 present (12.6 counts rendered avatars), the chip may not silently understate the room (count all or visibly mark overflow), and tracking is proven by backfill: killing a rendered peer promotes the unrendered ninth within ~10 s; solo-regression and build/console gates fold into AC6.
- **NEW in AC 6 (promoted from sprint-14 soft note 1):** the 390px chip-under-wordmark overlap is a recorded defect in exactly this sprint's "mobile with company" scope, so chip, wordmark, nav pills, hint, prompt, and touch cluster must be pairwise non-overlapping at 390x844 with a peer present, chip text legible on film.
