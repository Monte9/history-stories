# Sprint 12 criteria alignment — The body: third-person view

Aligned 2026-06-11 21:51 UTC (pre-build). Drafts reviewed against GOAL.md #1, SPEC 11 (11.2-11.5), SPEC 7, SPEC 3-4 shipped semantics, and RUBRIC "Criteria alignment". Six agreed ACs; sub-bullets are one verifiable check each. All checks are headless-verifiable via Playwright screenshots and `#museum-hud` attributes at 1280x900 and 390x844. Pose checks are phrased against `data-speed`/`data-x`/`data-z` displacement, not wall-clock animation timing, because the eval renderer is SwiftShader at ~2-10 fps.

## Agreed acceptance criteria

**AC1 — Third person is the default and the frame is composed.** A fresh session loading `/?face=roman` at 1280x900 reaches `data-cam-mode="third"` with `data-cam-dist` > 0, and the screenshot shows:
- a complete human figure seen from behind — head, torso, two arms, two legs — with distinct clothing tones (head/hands vs shirt vs trousers vs shoes), not a capsule, blob, or stick figure
- the body fully in frame occupying roughly the lower third of it, with the Roman wall and its paintings clearly visible and unobstructed beyond the figure
- the figure's silhouette clearly separable from the white wall and light floor behind it (no white-on-white wash-out in the bright room)
- a fresh 390x844 load shows the same third-person body with the touch cluster present and no horizontal overflow

**AC2 — Movement parity: every shipped input still works and the body follows.** Arrows/WASD, wheel, pointer drag, and the 390px touch cluster each still drive `data-x`/`data-z`/`data-heading`/`data-pitch` with their shipped semantics (a single `mouse.wheel(0, -300)` displaces the player 0.5-4 units; drag right increases heading; vertical drag clamps pitch to ±25°; collision clamp holds), and after each input the avatar renders at the new player position facing along `data-heading` in screenshots.

**AC3 — The gait reads human: never skates, never freezes, never a turntable statue.**
- while walking (`data-speed` ≥ ~2), two screenshots with the player displaced ~0.4 units apart show clearly different limb configurations — legs alternating, arms counter-swinging — and no pair of in-motion screenshots shows identical limbs while `data-x`/`data-z` changed (no skating)
- `data-speed` reads ~0.00 at rest and rises toward ~3.00 while holding forward
- after stopping (once `data-speed` reads 0.00), a screenshot taken within ~1 s shows a natural standing pose (feet under the body, arms hanging), not a frozen mid-stride pose
- turn-in-place: holding a turn key with no forward input changes `data-heading` while `data-speed` stays ~0, and screenshots at two different headings show the body rotated with visible foot/leg pose differences (not a rigid statue rotating)

**AC4 — Focus, Enter, Escape all work from third person, proving the aim is the player's.** At `/?face=roman` 1280x900: the wall-center painting sets `data-focused` and shows `#museum-prompt`, and in the screenshot that painting sits near the horizontal center of frame above the avatar's head; turning moves focus along the wall and aiming at empty wall clears `data-focused`; point-blank focus (standing at the wall facing a painting) still works; Enter opens the focused story; Escape returns to the same `data-x`/`data-z`/`data-heading` (± float noise). At 390x844 the tap-to-focus, tap-to-open path still works with the body in scene (the body never intercepts a tap).

**AC5 — The camera never leaves the room or blinds you.** Backing the player into each of the four walls and at least one corner: every screenshot shows only room interior (no void, no through-wall geometry; paintings, floor, and ceiling intact); `data-cam-dist` shrinks below its default while pinned and recovers to the default after walking back to open floor; when the boom is very short the local body fades or clears so the frame is not filled by the avatar's back.

**AC6 — Mode switching is complete and nothing shipped regresses.**
- pressing `V` flips `data-cam-mode` between `"third"` and `"first"`; the visible `#museum-cam-toggle` button does the same and is present and tappable at 390x844 (coarse pointer)
- in first person no part of the local body is visible at any heading/pitch, `data-cam-dist` reads `"0.00"`, and movement plus gaze focus behave exactly as shipped
- `?cam=first` forces first person for that load; a chosen mode survives a story round trip (Enter then Escape returns in the same mode); a subsequent `?face=` load preserves the stored mode (placement only, per SPEC 11.3)
- the no-WebGL fallback still shows its message and the "Gallery view" link with no console error spam; zero console errors in the room; no horizontal overflow at 390px; `pnpm build` exits 0 (hard gates restated for this sprint's scope)

## Changes vs draft, one-line rationale each

1. **Draft 1 → AC1 (strengthened).** Added SPEC 11.3's own lower-third composition bound, a bright-room silhouette-legibility check (white-on-white is the likely failure in the Broad room), and a fresh 390x844 framing check; kept the limb/clothing inventory and `data-cam-mode`.
2. **Draft 2 → AC2 (kept, tightened).** "Sprint-2/8 semantics" made measurable by citing the shipped tuning bounds (wheel displacement 0.5-4 u, pitch ±25°, collision clamp) so parity is a check, not a vibe.
3. **Draft 3 → AC3 (strengthened, re-phrased).** Replaced wall-clock "~300 ms apart" sampling with displacement-based sampling (SwiftShader runs 2-10 fps, so timed pose pairs are flaky) and added the turn-in-place check — GOAL's gait-while-turning demand and SPEC 11.2's turntable failure mode were uncovered by the drafts; kept idle-settle and no-skating.
4. **Draft 4 → AC4 (strengthened, deduplicated).** Added SPEC 11.4's observable contract (focused painting near horizontal frame center above the avatar's head — this is what proves player-aim rather than camera-aim), point-blank focus, and the 390px tap-through-body check; moved "Escape returns in the same camera mode" to AC6 to cut duplication with draft 6.
5. **Draft 5 → AC5 (kept, extended).** Added boom recovery after leaving the wall so the clamp is proven elastic, not a one-way ratchet; kept the four-walls-plus-corner sweep, no-void, and body-fade checks.
6. **Draft 6 → AC6 (restructured, extended).** Kept `V` / `#museum-cam-toggle` / `?cam=` / round-trip persistence; added `?face=` mode-preservation and first-person `data-cam-dist="0.00"` (SPEC 11.3/11.5 contract lines), and added the no-WebGL fallback regression, which the sprint goal names but no draft covered; console-errors and build stay as restated hard gates.

Nothing was cut outright; the only redundancy removed was the duplicated mode-persistence check between drafts 4 and 6. Count stays at six.
