6. **No regressions — FAIL.** The focus highlight does not render for the initially gaze-focused painting on any fresh load. Evidence:
   - Fresh `/?face=roman`: `data-focused="...marcus-aurelius..."`, `#museum-prompt` visible, but 0 amber outline pixels in the painting region at 1.5s and 3s after load (`s8-race-initial.png`, `s8-outline-zoom.png` shows bare dark frame on white wall). Walking to 2.3 units from the wall changes nothing (`s8-outline-close-1280.png`, edge crops `s8-close-leftedge.png` / `s8-close-bottomedge.png`: no outline, no emissive lift).
   - Turn away then back (focus soldier -> marcus): outline appears, 1472 amber px (`s8-race-refocus.png`). Same painting, same distance, same pitch. So the outline mesh and emissive lift work; they are skipped only for the first focus after mount.
   - Default spawn `/`: `data-focused="...king-who-chose-exile..."`, prompt visible, no green outline on the focused painting (`s8-spawn-initial-1280.png`, zoom `s8-spawn-zoom.png`; the greenish pixels in frame are the cover art and the RAMAYANA label, not a ring).
   - Why this is a regression: Sprint 3 AC2 and Sprint 7 AC3 (both PASSed, see `20260611-073109-sprint-7.md` "focused painting wears a clearly visible emerald outline") established the visible highlight as standing homepage behavior, and SPEC 4.2 requires the focused response be "clearly visible in a screenshot." Under the old proximity rule nothing was focused at mount, so first focus always happened after the paintings registered; the Sprint 8 gaze rule focuses a painting on frame 1 of every load, exposing a registration race: `FocusManager` (MuseumRoom.tsx ~line 273) applies `registry.get(best)?.(true)` once on transition, before `Painting`'s `useEffect` has put its `setFocus` in the registry, and the early-return `if (best === current.current) return;` means it is never re-applied. Net effect: on every first load the prompt claims focus while the room shows nothing, and Enter opens an unhighlighted painting.
